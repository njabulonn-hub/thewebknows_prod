About The Web Knows

Last updated: February 2024 · Author: The Web Knows team

What Is This?

The Web Knows is a diagnostics hub that shows you exactly what information websites can see about you—without forcing you to install extra software. The core scan runs inside your browser so you can audit your exposure in real time. To keep the tool free for everyone, we now display carefully reviewed advertising units; those partners may measure impressions or set cookies under their own privacy policies.

Why Does This Exist?

Many people don't realize how much information their browser automatically shares with websites. This tool helps you understand what's visible to any website you visit, so you can make informed decisions about your privacy.

If you're just getting started: lean on the plain-language summaries, everyday examples, and quick tips. They translate jargon like “ASN” or “Topics API” into scenarios you can recognise instantly.

If you work in security, marketing, or compliance: the same cards highlight legal obligations, adversarial abuse paths, and mitigation checklists. You can use them as shorthand for training teammates or auditing privacy controls.

How It Works

When you visit this site, your browser:

  • Makes requests to IP lookup services to find your public IP address and approximate location
  • Collects information about your browser, device, and system settings
  • Displays all of this information in plain English

All diagnostic logic executes locally. Advertising scripts load alongside the scan and may perform their own measurement or personalisation according to the controls you have with those partners.

Want to verify what is exposed right now? Launch The Web Knows privacy check and compare the live results with this overview. The diagnostic runs locally while ad placements load in parallel from our monetisation partners.

Data Practices & Advertising Support

We balance transparency with the reality that advertising keeps the service online:

  • Local diagnostics: Your scan results are rendered in the browser. We do not transmit the values we display back to our servers.
  • No first-party analytics: We do not run separate analytics pixels beyond what our ad partners require for billing and reporting.
  • Advertising scripts: Ad networks may load their own assets, set cookies, or read standard browser signals. Review each provider's privacy controls to manage those interactions.
  • Your choice: You can continue to use VPNs, content blockers, or browser privacy modes while running the diagnostic. Doing so may change both the scan results and the ads you see.
