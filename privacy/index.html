Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 2024

Use The Web Knows privacy diagnostic to confirm exactly what information is visible. The scan runs locally in your browser, while ad placements may load from our monetisation partners.

Our Commitment

The Web Knows is designed with privacy as its core principle. The diagnostic experience keeps calculations in your browser so you can see what the web sees without sending those results back to us. Advertising keeps the service sustainable, and we review partners for transparency before they are allowed on the page.

How the Diagnostic Works

When you visit this site your browser performs two sets of actions:

  • Local scan: Requests to IP lookup services (ipify.org, ipapi.co, ipinfo.io, dns.google) reveal your public network details. Browser APIs provide device, fingerprinting, and advertising status signals. These values are rendered directly to your screen.
  • Advertising load: Ad units are fetched from approved partner networks. They may set cookies or read browser identifiers to comply with their own frequency-capping and reporting obligations.

Reloading the page reruns the scan from scratch. We do not persist your diagnostic results between sessions.

What We Collect

  • Diagnostic results: Not collected or stored by us; the values live solely in your session.
  • Server logs: We retain minimal server logs for operational security (for example, aggregate error monitoring) and purge them on a rolling basis.
  • Advertising telemetry: Ad partners may gather impression counts, click data, or coarse device identifiers under their respective policies.

Advertising Partners

We work with privacy-reviewed ad networks to fund hosting and ongoing improvements. Each partner provides its own consent tools and opt-out mechanisms. Because ads are delivered from their infrastructure, they may set third-party cookies, use local storage, or read existing identifiers to measure reach.

For newcomers: Look for “Ad choices” or “Why this ad?” links inside the creative to manage settings quickly.

For advanced users: You can combine browser-based protections (content blockers, network filters, privacy modes) with the diagnostic to observe how ad signals respond.

Third-Party Services

To show you your IP address and location, we use the following services:

  • ipify.org — For IP address lookup (IPv4 and IPv6)
  • ipapi.co — For ISP and location information (primary)
  • ipinfo.io — For ISP and location information (fallback)
  • dns.google — For reverse DNS (PTR) lookups

These services may log your requests according to their own privacy policies. Advertising networks integrated on the site maintain separate privacy statements covering their telemetry. If you prefer not to use these services, you can disable JavaScript or rely on browser extensions to block the network calls.

Your Data

You can request removal of any server-side data we hold by contacting us; however, our operational logs are short-lived and do not include your diagnostic results. To manage advertising data, use the opt-out controls provided by each network or apply browser-level protections.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last updated" date.
